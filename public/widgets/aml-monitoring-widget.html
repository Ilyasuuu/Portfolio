Key AML Red Flags — Trade Finance

Sanctions exposure
Counterparty, country, or vessel appears on OFAC, EU, or UN sanctions lists.
Over/under-invoicing
Invoice value significantly diverges from market price — classic trade-based money laundering (TBML) technique.
Dual-use goods concern
Goods could have military/weapons application; requires enhanced due diligence under EU Dual-Use Regulation.
High-risk jurisdiction
FATF grey/black listed country, or jurisdiction with weak AML controls (e.g. Myanmar, Iran, North Korea).
Round-number amounts
Structured amounts (e.g. exactly $500,000) may indicate structuring to avoid reporting thresholds.
Shell company indicators
Newly incorporated entity, minimal web presence, registered agent address, mismatched business activity.
Third-party payment
Payment routed through a party not named in the LC or contract — potential layering indicator.
Multiple amendments
Unusual number of LC amendments may indicate fraudulent documentary adjustment.
